More Seasons Than Ted Lasso

We are back for our 4th season since our re-birth in 2019! Building on the success of our programs and feedback from the community, Galt FC will be adding additional programs for the 2023 season.

Letting kids be themselves at Galt FC, our team chant of "I got this! We got this!" reinforces kids' self-belief and reminds them that they are a part of something bigger, that together we can do anything. Whether learning how to do an elastico, pass to teammates or figure out how to work through losing the ball to another player and what it means to stay positive and play with a smile, little moments and the right set of values will help our players to navigate challenges on and off the pitch.

Lots of laughing, chasing, sprinkling grass, telling jokes and growing into awesome kids are daily session outcomes - and it's amazing. I can't believe how much the program has grown or express how incredible it really is to have the same families and players year-after-year grow our community and help to shape the tremendous outcomes we see in the players. The whole program was designed to make soccer accessible for families, with strong values of togetherness, resilience and active positivity helping to add a lot of hope fuel to Cambridge.

For those who have helped nurture and support the program over the last few years, I can't thank you enough for your trust in coaching your kids. For those who are considering joining for the first time, I can only offer that I will do everything I can to make this a great experience for your kids, families and community. Kindly, Mark Anderton Owner and Head Coach Galt Football Club

A Sustainability Mindset

The challenges and crises of Climate Change often seem a world away from Cambridge, but their impacts on our kids and future generations is an undeniable reality that all organizations need to address.

Last year we partnered with RARE to donate $200 for the planting of 10 trees, helping to better our local environment while at the same time acting to offset the carbon footprint of our Club. In 2023, we hope to further that partnership with RARE and help to create additional pathways of sustainability in Cambridge, for our Club, our players and our community.

Galt FC will always work to create a sustainable, environmentally-conscious and carbon neutral sports organization that models some of the best practices of other sports organizations globally.

The first steps toward that goal begin with our players and their families, as we will work to create a zero-waste season, meaning all water bottles should be reusable and all snacks should come in reusable containers too.

On a small-scale, every action we take will serve to better our community and contribute towards a mindset shift that will be hugely important in giving Climate Change a proper tackle.

THE ROOTS OF OUR CLUB

At a time when many of Europe's great football clubs were either in their infancy or yet to be formed, Galt Football Club was founded in Ontario in 1881. From a modest town came one of Canada's great sporting dynasties, as Galt went on to dominate the game throughout Ontario and nationally.

Touring Ontario and Manitoba in 1903, they were undefeated in 17 games. In 1904 Galt FC travelled to St. Louis to compete in the Olympic Games, where they won the gold medal. The following year they continued their hold on the game in Canada, defeating Montreal Westmount over two legs and claiming the title of National Champions.

In 1905 The Pilgrims - an English touring team of top amateurs - played in Canada and the United States, taking on Galt FC in Galt on September 17, 1905. The match was hyped as being for the "Championship of the World" as it pitted Olympic Champions Galt against top English players. The match was attended by an estimated 3500 people at Dickson Park, who saw a thrilling 3-3 draw between the English and Canadian giants.

The GALT Club

Galt Football Club's Youth Program operates as a social enterprise, transforming the traditional model of youth soccer into a community-based vehicle that makes sport more accessible for families. It brings us together at our roots.

Re-born in 2019 as a passion project, Galt Football Club has established itself as a community football - read soccer - organization with an emphasis on grassroots youth soccer. Every action, every step forward will emphasize community, sustainability and the pure joy that can be found in sharing time together on and off the ball.

Taking on best practices from grassroots development in Europe, as well as embracing the need for accessibility, Galt FC is beginning a new chapter by addressing some of the key issues facing sport in our community.

High registration fees can often hamper accessibility to youth soccer, forcing families and individuals into hard choices: swimming lessons or soccer; camping or soccer; new clothes for school or soccer. On its own, a registration fee may not seem an obstacle, but over the course of a summer or year's worth of budget decisions, it can be a challenging expense to absorb. Ensuring kids sports are more affordable just makes sense.
